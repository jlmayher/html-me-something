What is a story?

Stories are not merely a string of events. Stories have elements that make them distinct from a simple description of things that happened. Usually, stories have certain features.

  • Denouement
  • Middle
  • Plot
  • End
  • Beginning
  • Protaganist

    • My Brother to the Rescue

    I was at Nick's Bar-B-Que and Catfish in Carlyle, Arkansas with my parents and brother. We had just finished eating and were waiting up front to pay. I said to my mother,

    "I'm going to the restroom. Don't leave without me."

    When I came out of the restroom, my family was gone. I thought to myself "Not a big deal. They're probably just waiting outside." I went outside, looked arounnd, and didn't see them. Then, I saw them. Dad was driving down the road in the truck, My mother and brother we just leaving the parking lot in the car. Crap.

    I ran through the parking lot, heedless of the cars driving through. The only thing I could think was that I had to get Mom's attention or be stuck here. Unfortunately for me, she didn't notice me jumping up and down in the road in her rear-view mirror, and continued to drive off to my grandmother's house.

    Now I was worried. This was long enough ago that we didn't have cell phones that worked out of state, and the phone service to my grandmother's house had been turned off when she moved to the assisted-care facility. I really didn't know who to call. Our cousin lived next door to grandma's house, but even though I'd know her my entire life, I had no idea what her last name was (Thomas, I found out later it was Thomas).

    I slumped onto the bench in front of Nick's trying to decide if I should ask someone there for a ride out to my grandmother's house or try to walk the several miles there. Could I call the police? I was an adult, not a lost child. Just as I was about to walk inside and talk to someone, a familiar looking 1994 Chrysler New Yorker pulled up, rolled down the window and from inside someone called,

    "Need a ride somewhere?"

    It was my mother! I was saved from having to ask a total stranger for help because I didn't know my own cousin's last name. I very nearly fainted from relief.

    It turned out that both of my parents assumed I was riding with the other and the only reason Mom returned was that my brother saw me juping up and down in the road and asked our mom who I was riding with. When she told him I was with Dad, he said,

    "If she's riding with Dad, then why is she jumping up and down in the road behind us?"
